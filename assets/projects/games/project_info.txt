Short Description:
Description: Throughout my studies, I’ve developed several games—some as solo projects, others in groups or during fast-paced Game Jams. These projects were a great way to experiment with user experience in a playful context. I particularly enjoy tinkering with non-traditional interactions, such as gesture and motion detection via camera or exploring immersive environments in Virtual Reality.

While I’m not looking to pursue a career specifically in the gaming industry, these projects are a great showcase of my broader technical skillset. They represent my experience across the entire pipeline, from initial concept and UI design to 3D art and programming.I find that the constraints and 'juice' of game design often inform my approach to making traditional interfaces more engaging.

-

Lupos is a cooperative, two-player obstacle course designed during my Master’s program. In this game, players must work together to maneuver a character through a challenging world by using their bodies: leaning left or right to steer and raising their arms to jump. For the movements to register, both players have to sync their actions perfectly.

Developed in a team of four, this project was a significant technical challenge. We used Microsoft Kinect for motion and gesture tracking, integrated with Unity. My role focused on the general game design and leading the technical implementation. Seeing people genuinely enjoy the experience during our final Master’s exhibition was incredibly rewarding and proved how engaging physical interaction can be.

-

NecroQuest is a fast-paced typing game where players summon skeletons to defend against an approaching knight. The core mechanic relies on typing accuracy and speed, turning a simple input method into a survival challenge.

For this project, I wanted to explore the intersection of game art and generative AI. I used AI tools to generate the initial character and environment assets, then performed extensive refinements and touch-ups in Photoshop to ensure a cohesive visual style. This allowed me to act as both the developer and the art director, maintaining a high-quality aesthetic throughout the game.

-

Uhrturm Climber was developed for a course assignment focused on the theme of 'Invisibility.' I designed a 2D platformer featuring a unique mirrored-level mechanic: the platforms on the player's side are invisible, forcing them to navigate by looking at the visible reflection on the opposite side of the screen. This project was an exercise in spatial awareness and unconventional navigation, challenging the player to rely on visual cues rather than direct sight to complete the climb.

-

Anubis Escape Room was a VR experience developed during my Bachelor’s studies. The project places players inside an ancient Egyptian temple, challenging them to navigate the environment and solve puzzles to find their way out. This project allowed me to explore the unique constraints of spatial UI and 3D interaction, focusing on how users manipulate objects and move through a virtual space without breaking immersion.

-

Dogma Hypnos is a high-fidelity UI prototype for a sci-fi space exploration game. The goal of this project was to design and build a fully functional main menu and settings system, moving beyond static layouts to create a reactive, animated experience. I focused on developing a cohesive futuristic aesthetic while ensuring the navigation was intuitive. Every element was fully interactive and animated, allowing me to explore how motion design can provide user feedback and enhance the overall atmosphere of a digital interface.

-

Developed for the HTC Vive, this project is a third-person VR platformer where the player must guide a turtle through a treacherous labyrinth. The gameplay focuses on environmental puzzle-solving—clearing obstacles and defeating enemies to ensure the turtle's safe passage. Built in Unity, the project explored the challenges of maintaining player comfort and orientation in a 3D VR space. The final game was selected for exhibition at the 'Mixed Reality Day' at FH Hagenberg.
