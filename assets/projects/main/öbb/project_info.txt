Description: During my Bachelor’s studies, I completed a six-month internship at ÖV Ticketshop GmbH (ÖBB). This experience was pivotal in solidifying my passion for UX, as it allowed me to contribute to a wide array of high-impact projects within a multidisciplinary team.

My responsibilities spanned the entire design lifecycle, including conceptualization, web design, and information architecture. A significant part of my role involved hands-on research, where I conducted user interviews and live usability tests at Vienna Central Station.

Working within a large corporate structure, I gained deep insights into professional workflows and Agile methodologies like Scrum. This internship taught me how to balance user needs with complex business requirements while collaborating effectively in a fast-paced environment.

-

One of my primary responsibilities was designing and developing a corporate website for ÖV Ticketshop to establish their external brand presence. I began the process with a competitive analysis and hand-drawn wireframes to ensure a logical information architecture.

While the visual language stayed true to the established ÖBB design system, I intentionally integrated a modern, minimalist aesthetic. After the design phase, I developed the site using HTML and CSS, which served as the technical foundation for the final product. Much of the current live site still retains my original layout and copy. This project was a significant learning experience, strengthening both my technical development skills and my ability to communicate design decisions within a professional team.

-

The website was designed with a desktop-first approach, utilizing a clearly structured visual language. During the prototyping phase, I used strategic placeholders to define the layout before final assets were integrated. To ensure a seamless experience across devices, I also developed a comprehensive mobile version, ensuring the minimalist design remained functional and legible on smaller screens.

In addition to digital interfaces, I contributed to the visual design of the ÖBB Nightjet Premium Tickets. Being involved in such a high-profile, physical touchpoint allowed me to apply brand guidelines to a premium user experience, ensuring that the luxury of the service was reflected in the ticket design itself.

-

A major focus of my internship was the redesign of the mobile ticketing interface. I was responsible for developing concepts and animations to enhance the visual feedback of the digital ticket. My creative explorations ranged from clock-based motifs and journey progress indicators to stylized icons of buses and trains.

Using Adobe After Effects, I created high-fidelity animations that served as functional prototypes for the final implementation. This process allowed the team to visualize the motion language and user interaction before moving into full production.
