Description: My Master’s thesis explored how modern technology can bridge the gap between clinical physiotherapy and at-home recovery. Following a rigorous Design Thinking process, I developed a dual-sided ecosystem designed to support both patients and therapists through a continuous feedback loop.

The Research: > To truly understand the needs of my users, I moved beyond theory and stepped into their shoes. I conducted expert interviews and synthesized my findings using Empathy Maps, Personas, and Interaction Storyboards. This iterative research phase was crucial in uncovering the real-world frustrations of physical recovery.

The Solution: > The final concept consists of a mobile app for patients and a web dashboard for therapists. The mobile application utilizes AI-driven body tracking to monitor exercises and provide real-time postural feedback. These data points are then synced to the web application, allowing therapists to monitor progress remotely and adjust treatment plans accordingly.

The Outcome: > By creating a functional technical prototype, I was able to demonstrate how a data-driven approach can increase patient motivation and provide therapists with more accurate insights between in-person sessions.

-

Based on these insights, I architected comprehensive user flows and interaction diagrams to ensure a seamless logic across the entire experience.

-

The UI for both the web and mobile applications was designed in Figma, with a focus on creating a modern, cohesive visual identity across the entire ecosystem.

-

The technical prototype was developed primarily to demonstrate feasibility. By integrating Google MediaPipe, I was able to use a standard smartphone camera to track various exercises and calculate the quality of a user's movements in real-time. To provide this instant feedback, I developed the application as a web app using Visual Studio Code.

-

Reflecting on this journey, I found immense value in deep-diving into a complex, real-world challenge. Engaging directly with experts and users not only refined the project’s direction but also reinforced my passion for research-driven design. This experience taught me how to translate specialized professional needs into intuitive digital tools, and I truly enjoyed the process of turning expert insights into a meaningful, technical solution.
